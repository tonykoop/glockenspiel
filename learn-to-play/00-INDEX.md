# Glockenspiel — Starter Songbook

The glockenspiel is a soprano metallophone with steel bars struck with hard mallets.
Its bright, bell-like tone projects easily and decays quickly — ideal for clear, stepwise melodies.

## Instrument range
- Beginner range used here: **C5–C7** (c' to c'' in ABC notation)
- Full instrument range: typically C5–C8 depending on model

## ABC notation note
All tunes are written at concert (sounding) pitch. Notes in the `c'` octave sound at C5.
A glockenspiel pitched "two octaves above written" in orchestral parts would require
transposing notation — this songbook writes what you **actually hear**.

## Difficulty key
- ★☆☆ — Beginner: conjunct motion, one octave range
- ★★☆ — Intermediate: skips of a third or fifth, two octaves
- ★★★ — Advanced: leaps, ornaments, fast passage work

## Mallet guidance
- Use hard rubber or plastic mallets for bright tone.
- Strike each bar at its center, not the node holes at the ends.
- Let the mallet rebound — don't press down after striking.
