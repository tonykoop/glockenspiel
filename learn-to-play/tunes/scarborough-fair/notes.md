# Notes: Scarborough Fair

**Provenance:** Traditional English ballad, origin unclear but documented from at least the 17th century.
Multiple versions in Cecil Sharp's English Folk Songs (1907, PD). This arrangement is freshly written
from the traditional lead-sheet melody — no Simon & Garfunkel arrangement rights (their arrangement
from 1966 is copyrighted, but the underlying traditional melody is PD).

**Range check:** C5–D6. The D6 is the highest note — it occurs early in each verse.

**Practice tips:**
1. The D dorian mode means no key signature — play all notes natural.
2. The octave leap (a' to d'') at the start is characteristic — don't rush it.
3. At ♩= 76, count a gentle 1-2-3 waltz pulse; never speed up.
